Study Guide: Big Idea 5

The Laws of Thermodyamics

  • The First Law of Thermodynamics states that energy can be transformed from one form to another, but it can be neither created nor destroyed.
  • The Second Law of Thermodynamics states that reactions are spontaneous when the total entropy of the universe increases.

Gibbs Free Energy

  • Use the ∆G = ∆H - T∆S to find if the reaction is spontaneous or not.
  • When ∆G is negative, that means the reaction is spontaneous.
  • When ∆H is negatve and ∆S is positive, then ∆G would be negative at all temperatures and the reaction would be spontaneous.
  • ∆G changes with temperature. Let's say that ∆H is positive and ∆S is also positive, then the reaction would be spontaneous at only high temperatures.

Standard Conditions

  • Standard Conditions: 1 atm, 1M, and 298 K

Enthalpy

Enthalpy Change of Formation

  • Enthalpy change of formation is when one mole of a substance is formed from its elements in their standard states
  • Use ΔH (reaction) = ∑ΔH (products) − ∑ΔH (Reactants) equation to find the overall ΔH of formation of the reaction. Notice this equation only works with ΔH of formation.

Enthalpy Change of Combustion

  • Enthalpy change of combustion is when one mole of a substance is completely burned in oxygen.
  • To find the ΔH of combustion, you will be provided with a series of reactions with their either ΔHf or ΔHc. Add all the ΔH together and change the sign if one compound is on the reactant side, but it is on the product side of the overall reaction (and vice versa).

Hess' Law

  • Hess’ Law: The enthalpy change during a reaction depends only on the nature of the reactants and products and is independent of the route taken
  • Single elements have zero as their ΔHf because they have not reacted with anything yet to be a compound. Oxygen always has a zero for both, the ΔHf and ΔHc.

Exothermic vs Endothermic

  • Exothermic is the releasing of energy or heat.
  • Endothermic is the absorbing of energy or heat.
  • Breaking bonds results in an endothermic reaction while forming them results in an exothermic reation.

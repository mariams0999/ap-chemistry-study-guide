Study Guide: Big Idea 1

Subatomic Particles

Particles Charge Mass in amu Position in the atom
Proton Positive Charge 1 amu Inside of the Nucleus of an atom
Electron Negative Charge 0 amu In the orbitals around the Nucleus (on the energy levels/clouds)
Neutron Neutral Charge (No Charge) 1 amu Inside of the Nucleus of an atom along with the Proton

Electron Configurations

  • Each s shell has room for one orbital with two electrons.
  • Each p shell has room for three orbitals with six electrons.
  • Each d shell has room for five orbitals with ten electrons.
  • Each f shell has room for seven orbitals with fourteen electrons.

Periodic Trends

Ionization Energy

  • Ionization energy is the energy required to remove an electron from an atom.
  • Ionization energy increases as you move to the right across a period. That's becuase as you move to the right, the number of protons within elements increases which increases the nuclear charge of the elements. This makes the nucleus be more attracted to electrons which increases an element's ionization energy.
  • Ionization energy decreases as you go down a group. That's because as you move down, a new shell gets added. This additional shell gives the valence electrons sheilding from the nucleus attraction by the inner electrons. This makes it easier for an electron to be removed, lowering the ionization energy of an element.

Electronegativity

  • Electronegativity is a measure of the tendency of an atom to attract electrons.
  • Electronegativity increases as you move to the right across a period because as you move to the right, the number of protons inreases, increasing the nuclear charge of elements. This increase brings about an attraction between the nucleus and electrons, increasing the electronegativity of elements
  • Electronegativity decreases as you go down a group because as you move down, a new n shell is added which makes it harder for electrons to be attracted by the nucleus. The inner electrons shield any incomming electrons, so they fell less attraction by the nucleus. This decreases the electronegativity of elements.

Atomic Radius

  • The atomic radius is a measure of the size of atoms, usually the mean or typical distance from the center of the nucleus to the boundary of the surrounding shells of electrons.
  • Atomic Radius decreases as you move to the right across a period because as you move to the right, the number of protons inreases, increasing the nuclear charge of elements. This increase in nuclear charge increases the attraction between the nucleus and the electrons. This makes the nucleus have a strong pull of electrons, decreasing an element's atomic radius.
  • Atomic Radius increases as you go down a group. That's because as you move down, the number of n shells increases which gives elements' a larger atomic radius.
